Analysis of Indian Other Electrical Equipment Sector

The Indian Other Electrical Equipment sector (encompassing conductors, cables, solar modules/cells, power infrastructure, EV chargers, stabilizers, and related products) is experiencing robust structural growth driven by renewable energy (RE) expansion, grid modernization, and export opportunities. Insights are derived from Q2/H1 FY26 earnings transcripts (e.g., APAR Industries, Saatvik Green Energy, Servotech) and investor meet announcements (e.g., Premier Energies, Waaree Energies, Genus Power). Key players like APAR (conductors/cables/oils), Saatvik/Premier/Vikram/Waaree (solar), and Servotech (EV/solar/BESS) highlight a mix of strong fundamentals amid short-term challenges.

Tailwinds

  • RE Boom & Govt Push: Record 25GW RE capacity addition in H1 FY26 (21.7GW solar). Targets like 500GW non-fossil by 2030, PM Surya Ghar, CPSU Scheme-II, and hydro/transmission plans (e.g., Rs. 6.4Tn for 76GW evacuation) drive demand for conductors, cables, modules, and substations (74% YoY addition).
  • Domestic Strength: Substation growth (44k MVA added), reconductoring (HTLS conductors for 100-200% capacity uplift), data centers, and wind/solar projects. H2 execution improves post-monsoon.
  • Exports & Premium Mix: US demand (data centers, wind/solar) up 145% (APAR conductors), exports 34.7% of revenue. Premium products (45% mix) yield higher EBITDA (Rs. 39-41k/MT).
  • Backward Integration & Capex: Solar firms expanding cells/modules (e.g., Saatvik 8.8GW modules + 4.8GW cells by FY27; APAR Rs. 1300cr capex). BESS, inverters, pumps emerging.
  • Policy Support: ALMM, PLI schemes favor domestic manufacturing.

Headwinds

  • Commodity Volatility: Al prices at $2900/MT, Cu at $11,200+/MT – global ordering freeze as EPCs/developers wait for correction.
  • US Tariffs/Geopolitics: Section 232 (50% on Al/Cu/steel), reciprocal duties (India 54% vs. ME/UK 10%) caused Q2 order halt (2 months). Impacts non-metal portion (30-50% value).
  • Execution Delays: Monsoon (Q2 sales deferral), right-of-way issues, milestone billing. GST cut (modules 12%→5%) deferred Q2 billing (e.g., Saatvik sales down QoQ).
  • EV Slowdown: Subsidies withdrawn; OMCs (HPCL/BPCL/Adani) paused projects post-6,500 installations.
  • Flat Segments: Oils flat due to lower crude; transmission lines lag (27% YoY decline).

Growth Prospects

  • Revenue Trajectory: APAR H1 revenue Rs. 10,820cr (+25% YoY, first >10k half); Saatvik Rs. 1,684cr (+133%). Cables/conductors 25-39% growth; solar orders strong (Saatvik 4.68GW book).
  • Order Visibility: APAR Rs. 7k+cr (conductors), Rs. 1.8kcr (cables); repeat US/domestic orders. H2 ramp-up (post-Diwali, incentives deadline Jul’26).
  • Capex-Driven Scale: Rs. 1,300cr+ FY26 (APAR); solar to 8-10GW+ capacities. EBITDA guidance stable (conductors Rs. 30k/MT; cables 10-12%).
  • Diversification: BESS/EV (Servotech tie-ups), reconductoring (urban/data centers), exports beyond US (Europe/LatAm/Canada). FY26 growth 25-88% CAGR sustained.
  • Medium-Term: RE demand (5-6% energy CAGR), grid augmentation, RTC projects position sector for 20-30%+ annual growth.

Key Risks

  • Short-Term Pressure: Q3 topline/EBITDA dip (US execution lag, metal prices); H1FY26-like moderation if tariffs persist.
  • Policy/External Shocks: US trade deal delays, subsidy reversals (EV/BESS), forex fluctuations.
  • Competition/Overcapacity: Solar module flood (Mono PERC obsolescence → N-TOPCon shift); new entrants erode realizations ($0.145-0.16/W).
  • Execution/Supply Chain: Weather/ROW delays, capex overruns (Odisha/Falta plants on track but ramp-up risks).
  • Margin Compression: Metal pass-through hedged, but non-metal duties, overheads (e.g., Saatvik Q2 EBITDA dip to 16%).

Summary

The sector is bullish long-term (RE/grid tailwinds, capex cycle) with 20-30% growth potential FY26-28, led by solar (modules/cells) and T&D (conductors/cables). Short-term caution (Q3 softness from metals/tariffs ~10-20% revenue risk) offset by strong books (90%+ capacity utilization) and H2 recovery. Risk-reward favors bulls – monitor US tariffs, metal prices, and execution. Valuations may consolidate near-term but offer entry for 2-3yr horizon (EBITDA margins 9-18%, ROCE 20%+). Diversified players (APAR) resilient vs. solar pure-plays.
