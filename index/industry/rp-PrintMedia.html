Indian Print Media Sector Analysis (Based on Q2 FY26 Earnings Transcripts of D B Corp Ltd and HT Media/HMVL)

The transcripts from D B Corp Ltd (Dainik Bhaskar group, primarily Hindi print) and HT Media Group (Hindustan Times English + Hindustan Hindi) highlight a resilient yet challenged sector. Q2 FY26 showed broad-based print ad growth (9-12% YoY) driven by festive tailwinds and cost efficiencies, but moderated H1 growth (2-4% revenue). Print remains core (80-90% revenue), with radio/digital as growth bets amid stable newsprint (~Rs 47k/MT). Below is a structured analysis of headwinds, tailwinds, growth prospects, and key risks.

Tailwinds (Positive Supports)

  • Soft & Stable Newsprint Prices: Both firms noted prices at cycle lows (Rs 47k/MT for DB Corp; lowest quartile per HT). Expected range-bound/gradual rise aids margins (DB EBITDA +10%, HT Print EBITDA doubled YoY with +500 bps expansion).
  • Festive & Macro Boost: Early Navratri (Sep 22) drove ad growth ex-like-for-like (high single-digit). GST cuts spurred auto/electronics (double-digit growth); normal monsoon/GDP aided recovery.
  • Ad Category Strength: Real estate, auto, jewelry, education, health, banking grew double-digits (DB: all except govt/FMCG; HT: all commercial + govt in Hindi). DB ad +12% YoY; HT Print +10%.
  • Circulation Resilience: Steady traction (DB +3% revenue, maintained ~40L copies vs industry +2.7%; HT copies up YoY/QoQ despite pricing). DB #1 per ABC (Jan-Jul 2025).
  • Cost Discipline: Events/promos expensed but revenue captured in ads; FOREX/CSR one-offs adjusted for strong PAT growth (DB +13%).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Weak Key Ad Categories: Govt ads declined sharply (DB: -12-13%, mix 17% vs 25% YoY; HT Hindi positive but overall radio stress). FMCG flat/declining (DB +5%, H1 decline).
  • Circulation Stagnation: Despite ground efforts (promos, schemes, surveys), copies maintained not grown (DB: “disheartened” in Bihar/Jharkhand/Maharashtra; HT optimizing market-by-market with pricing discounts).
  • Radio Woes: Industry duress; DB +4% but EBITDA flat; HT revenue flat YoY (loss -INR 4cr), 90% of HT standalone impairment (INR 38cr) from radio investments.
  • Digital Investment Drag: Scaling suppresses margins (DB: 20mn MAU, leadership in Hindi/Guj; HT OTTplay: +10% revenue but -INR 30cr loss, timing mismatch in costs/revenue).
  • High Receivables: Govt delays (6-24 months, 30% >6 months per DB), persistent issue despite representations.

Growth Prospects

  • Festive Momentum (Q3/Q4): Oct-Nov festivals + Bihar elections (DB/HT expect sustained auto; real estate lag but potential from GST/stamp duty cuts).
  • Digital Scaling: DB: 20mn MAU, state apps (Uttarakhand launch), hyper-local content. HT: OTTplay subs surge (Sep CAC drop, sticky renewals), Hindi/Guj leadership. Potential monetization as scale builds (universe 12-13cr smartphone users).
  • Radio Expansion: DB adding 14 stations (to 44 by Mar 2026, exclusive in 7); TRAI push for radio in mobiles (emergency alerts).
  • Print Yield/Volume Mix: Mostly volume-led (+1-2% yield); potential pricing post-circ growth. Structural Hindi strength (DB/HT +13% ad).
  • Efficiency Gains: Stable inputs + events/integration (DB radio/digital synergies; HT integrated formats).

Key Risks

  • Input Cost Volatility: Newsprint rise (currency fluctuations; HT: gradual per RISI). No cover price hikes planned (DB prioritizes volumes).
  • Ad Mix Dependency: Govt/FMCG vulnerability (elections code-of-conduct negative in Q3 per HT); real estate slow pass-through.
  • Circulation Pressure: Tier-2/3/4 competition (metros reviving pullouts like Mumbai Mirror/City Bhaskar); promo spends not yielding outsized growth.
  • Digital Path to Profit: Losses rising short-term (HT timing issues; DB pre-monetization learnings); uncertain CAC sustainability.
  • Macro/Regulatory: Election cycles, delayed payments, no MF interest in print (HT: traditional media less favored). Radio implementation delays.

Overall Summary

Indian print media delivered a “decent” Q2 (DB: 9-13% growth; HT: 4-10%), buoyed by festive/GST tailwinds and newsprint stability, but H1 softened (2-4% revenue) due to govt/FMCG weakness and radio/digital drags. Print core intact (leadership, margins up), with digital/radio as multi-year bets. Outlook mildly positive for H2 (festivals, expansions), targeting high single-digits ex-festive, but risks from inputs, payments, and category softness cap upside. Sector resilience hinges on cost control, digital inflection, and ad recovery—print not fading, but evolving hybrid model essential.

Indian Print Media Sector Analysis (Q3 & 9M FY25 Insights from DB Corp, JPL, HMVL, HT Media)

The Indian print media sector showed resilience amid challenges in Q3 FY25 (ended Dec 31, 2024). Leaders like DB Corp reported record 9M revenue (₹18,544 Mn, +1% YoY) and PAT (₹3,187 Mn, +5% YoY), driven by print ad growth and cost efficiencies. JPL sustained strong revenue (consol Q3 ₹51,650 Mn), with print profitability. HMVL achieved a turnaround (9M PAT ₹3,238 Lakhs vs prior loss), aided by EBITDA positivity. HT Media (group level) remained loss-making (9M loss ₹3,716 Lakhs), reflecting digital/radio drags. Overall, print remains core (70-80% revenue) but flat YoY; digital/radio investments weigh on profitability. Key metrics: Ad revenue stable/soft post-elections; newsprint costs down 10-14% YoY; EBITDA margins improving (DB Corp print at 32%, +200 bps).

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Cost Efficiencies: Newsprint prices soft/stable (DB Corp: avg. ₹47,600 PMT, -14% YoY), boosting margins. Effective controls lifted DB Corp EBITDA +4% YoY; HMVL EBITDA ₹5,607 Lakhs.
  • Print Ad Resilience: Festive demand strong; DB Corp ad CAGR 20% (FY21-24); JPL print revenue ~76% of total. Regional leadership (DB Corp: 6.67 Cr readership across 12 states).
  • Digital Momentum: DB Corp apps #1 in Hindi/Gujarati (MAU 15.7 Mn Oct’24, +9x since 2020); hyperlocal content/user engagement rising.
  • Diversification: Radio growth (DB Corp: +9% ad rev to ₹1,287 Mn); events/CSR boosting engagement (e.g., DB Corp Jeeto 14 Cr scheme).
  • EBITDA Recovery: Sector-wide uptick (DB Corp 29%; HMVL positive vs prior negativity).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • High Election Base: Q3 ad revenues flat/declining YoY (DB Corp consol rev -1.4%; JPL flat; HMVL print down slightly) due to prior state elections.
  • Digital/Radio Losses: Persistent drags (JPL radio loss ₹209 Mn Q3; HMVL digital loss ₹7,165 Lakhs 9M; HT Media digital/radio negative).
  • Revenue Pressure: Overall topline muted (+1% avg 9M YoY); post-festive ad softening.
  • High Fixed Costs: Employee/depreciation steady (30-40% expenses); impairment losses elevated (JPL ₹935 Lakhs Q3).
  • Litigation/Claims: Promoter disputes (JPL NCLT petition); wage claims (JPL ₹116 Cr pending); radio licensing issues (JPL Music Broadcast).

Growth Prospects

  • Digital Pivot: Phygital leadership (DB Corp #1 apps); personalized content/videos; monetization via premium journalism (target: engaged users > willingness to pay).
  • Regional Expansion: Multi-language dominance (Hindi/Gujarati/Marathi); radio scale-up (DB Corp EBITDA +11%).
  • Ad Recovery: Optimistic sentiments; traditional sectors + elections/diversity. DB Corp targets sustained CAGR via market leadership.
  • Efficiency Gains: Newsprint stability expected 2-3 Qtrs; cost mgmt (print margins 30%+ potential).
  • New Streams: Events (MY FM Garba/Diwali); apps/videos for 19 Mn+ users (DB Corp).

Key Risks

  • Ad Cyclicality: Macro slowdown/post-festive dip; election dependency (high base persists).
  • Digital Monetization: Heavy losses (20-50% rev but EBITDA negative); competition from aggregators/apps.
  • Legal/Regulatory: Promoter litigations (JPL impact uncertain); wage/music disputes (potential outflows ₹100-1,000 Cr); tax changes (e.g., JPL MAT credits).
  • Newsprint Volatility: Dollar fluctuations could reverse savings.
  • Debt/ Liquidity: Elevated finance costs (5-10% rev); JPL/HT Media debt ratios ~1x; CP outstanding (HT ₹1,500 Cr).
  • Sector Structural: Print circulation flat (-0.4% DB Corp); shift to digital erodes margins without scale.

Summary Outlook: Moderate growth (5-10% topline FY25) anchored by print stability/digital upside, but profitability hinges on ad rebound/cost discipline. DB Corp outperforms as leader; others lag on diversification. Risks tilted toward execution (digital ROI) and macros; tailwinds from costs/digital provide buffers. Sector PE ~15-20x; watch Q4 ads/elections.

Indian Print Media Sector Analysis (Based on Q2FY26 Results of Key Players: Jagran Prakashan Ltd (JPL), Sandesh Ltd, and Contextual Insights from HMVL/HT Media)

The analysis draws from unaudited Q2/H1FY26 financials and disclosures of JPL (diversified print leader) and Sandesh (Gujarati print-focused with investments), reflecting broader sector trends. Print remains core (70-80% revenue for most), but diversification into digital/radio/OOH is accelerating amid digital disruption.

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Resilient Print Advertising: JPL’s print ad revenue +14% YoY (Dainik Jagran +17%), driven by pages-per-copy increase and product enhancements. Sandesh media revenue +11% YoY H1. Sector benefits from stable Hindi/Gujarati regional leadership (JPL: 6.9Cr readership; Sandesh: strong Gujarat presence).
  • Strong Balance Sheets & Liquidity: JPL net cash >₹1,000 Cr; Sandesh ₹85,000+ Cr investments, minimal debt. Enables dividends/buybacks (JPL: ₹1,900 Cr total), capex, and digital bets.
  • Cost Efficiencies: Newsprint stabilization, prudent controls boosted JPL print OPM to 20.3% (+150 bps YoY); Sandesh shifted Mid-Day to profitability.
  • Diversification Momentum: JPL OOH/Events +16% YoY; Radio City 18% market share, 29% “created business”. Digital reach ~65M users (top-15 news category).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Circulation Erosion: JPL print circulation revenue -1-2% YoY; expected H2 recovery via initiatives, but signals subscriber shift to digital/free content.
  • Radio & Digital Losses: JPL Radio OPM -14.5% (revenue -YoY); Digital OPM -13% despite user growth. Sandesh digital subsidiary losses (₹42-72L Q2/H1).
  • Segment Volatility: JPL consolidated OPM dipped to 14% (-87 bps YoY); Sandesh Q2 profit down sharply due to FVTPL investment swings (net losses in other expenses).
  • Macro Ad Pressure: Festive/peak season muted; over-reliance on ads (70-80% revenue), vulnerable to economic slowdowns.

Growth Prospects

  • Digital Monetization: JPL’s 19 portals, 65M+ users; Sandesh digital sub; partnerships (Google/Meta/Amazon). Revenue alignment with users expected as investments mature.
  • Non-Print Scale-Up: JPL OOH/Events as “value creators”; Radio integration with digital. Multi-platform (print+300 editions, 39 radio stations) for cross-sell.
  • Regional Dominance: JPL/Sandesh leadership in Hindi/Gujarati (83%+ readers); circulation stabilization via enhancements.
  • Capex & Innovation: JPL print initiatives; awards (e.g., E4M, CIO 100) signal tech/content edge. H1FY26 revenue +4-11% sets base for FY26 growth >10% if ads rebound.

Key Risks

Ad Cyclicality Economic slowdown, festive misses; investment volatility (Sandesh FVTPL swings). Medium-High; resilient core but OPM volatility.

Regulatory/Compliance SEBI LODR filings, AGM e-voting; leadership rumors (HT clarification). Low; strong governance.

Tailwinds from efficiencies/regional moats support mid-single digit growth; prospects hinge on digital monetization (high potential, 20-30% user base expansion). Risks center on ad dependency/digital transition—sector likely 5-10% FY26 growth, with leaders like JPL (print+digital) outperforming pure-plays. Monitor H2 circulation/digital EBITDA for inflection.

Indian Print Media Sector Analysis (Based on Q2 FY26 Earnings Transcripts of DB Corp Ltd and HT Media Group)

The analysis draws from the Q2 FY26 (ended Sep 30, 2025) conference calls of DB Corp Ltd (Dainik Bhaskar group) and HT Media Group (Hindustan Times/Hindustan), representing key Hindi/English print players. Both reported resilient print performance amid macro tailwinds, but face segment-specific challenges. Overall sector shows modest recovery post-Q1 softness, with print ads driving growth.

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Soft Newsprint Prices: Both companies highlighted stable/low prices (~Rs 47k/MT for DB; lowest quartile per HT). DB expects range-bound prices; HT notes gradual rise but sufficient inventory. This boosted EBITDA margins (DB: 10% YoY; HT Print: nearly doubled).
  • Advertising Recovery: Print ad growth strong (DB: +12% YoY, high single-digit LFL; HT: +10% YoY). Key sectors like auto, real estate, jewelry, education, health, banking grew double-digits (DB). Early Navratri/festives pulled demand forward; GST cuts spurred auto/electronics.
  • Circulation Resilience: Industry +2.7% (ABC Jan-Jul 2025); DB maintained ~40L copies (+3% revenue); HT reported copy growth (YoY/QoQ) despite pricing discounts for market expansion. DB leads as largest group.
  • Digital Momentum: DB: 20M MAU (Hindi/Gujarati leader), new Uttarakhand app. HT’s OTTplay: +10% revenue, strong Sep subscriptions/renewals, lower CAC.
  • Macro Support: Normal monsoon, GDP growth, interest rate cuts, GST slabs reduction (DB/HT).

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Government Ad Weakness: Major drag (DB: share fell to 17% from 25%, -12-13% YoY; HT Hindi: growth but implied pressure). Elections/new govt delayed spends; receivables >6 months persistent (govt/panchayat dues up to 1-2 yrs).
  • FMCG Slump: Flat/low growth (DB: +5%; both noted weakness).
  • Radio Distress: Under duress industry-wide (DB: EBITDA flat; HT: revenue flat YoY, -4cr EBITDA, radio impairments ~90% of HT’s 38cr exceptional loss).
  • Circulation Revenue Muted: Copies stable/growing, but revenue flat/declining (HT English: -15% YoY due to pricing for ramp-up; DB: efforts yielded maintenance, not outperformance).
  • Cost Pressures: DB: Elevated “other expenses” (CSR, events, promotions). HT: Digital/OTT costs timing mismatch led to higher losses (-30cr EBITDA).

Growth Prospects

  • Short-Term (Q3/Q4 FY26): Festivals, Bihar elections (pre-code benefits for Hindi), GST-led auto/real estate rebound (DB expects real estate “firing”; HT: half structural). DB radio expansion (14 new stations by Mar 26, sole private FM in 7). HT: Print margins expansion, digital scaling.
  • Medium-Term: Print leadership (DB #1 group; HT market-by-market optimization). Digital hyper-local/content innovation (DB: 12-13cr potential users; HT: OTT sticky subs). Radio revival via integrated formats/stations (DB: untapped markets; govt push for phone radio chips).
  • Overall Outlook: High single-digit print ad growth targeted (DB); sequential momentum (both +9-13% QoQ ads). Healthy cash (HT: 947cr net cash), prudent dividends (DB: ~58% payout).

Key Risks

Risk Category Details Impact
Input Cost Volatility Newsprint currency-linked; potential gradual rise (HT RISI forecast). Margin erosion if INR weakens.
Ad Mix Dependency Govt/FMCG ~20% share; election codes (Bihar Q3 negative for HT). Revenue volatility; delayed payments strain cash.
Digital Monetization High losses (HT OTT: -30cr); CAC sustainability unproven. Capex drag; scaling without profitability.
Circulation Competition Tier-2/3 stagnation despite efforts (DB: Bihar/Jharkhand weak; HT: pricing discounts). Metro revival (e.g., Mumbai Mirror) spillover uncertain. Share loss to digital/competitors.
Radio/Regulatory Industry duress; impairments (HT); TRAI radio-in-phones delayed. Segment value erosion.
Macro/Event-Driven Post-festive slowdown; real estate stamp duty unchanged (DB). Q3 weakness if pent-up demand fades.

Summary

Indian print media exhibits resilience with cautious optimism: Print core stable (ad +10-12% YoY, margins up on low newsprint), aided by macro tailwinds (GST/festives) and digital traction. However, headwinds from govt/FMCG weakness and radio losses cap upside. Growth prospects hinge on sectoral recovery (auto/real estate) and digital/radio scale-up, targeting high single-digits. Key risks center on ad volatility, costs, and monetization delays—watch Q3 for festive/Bihar election impact. Sector PE attractiveness may improve with profitability focus, but investor caution persists (e.g., HT MF absence). Overall: Stable FY26 with 5-10% revenue growth potential, assuming stable inputs.

Analysis of Indian Print Media Sector

Based on the provided documents (AGM notices, voting results, financial statements, and MD&A from key players like DB Corp Ltd (Dainik Bhaskar), Jagran Prakashan Ltd (Dainik Jagran), Sandesh Ltd, Hindustan Media Ventures Ltd, and HT Media Ltd), here’s a summary analysis of the Indian Print Media sector. Insights are drawn from FY 2024-25 financials (e.g., flat/declining revenues, profit drops due to lower investment gains), sector overviews (e.g., EY reports), and MD&A discussions (e.g., Sandesh’s detailed outlook).

Headwinds (Challenges)
  • Digital Disruption & Readership Decline: Print revenues flat/growing only 1% in 2024 (₹260B), lagging digital (17% growth). Circulation/subscriptions not recovering to pre-COVID levels; younger audiences shifting to social media/video (456M digital news users). Ad spends stagnant/down 6% for TV/print.
  • Revenue Pressure: Ad revenues ~14% below 2019 levels; dependency on print (majority revenue) amid SME/national shifts to digital. FY25 saw operational revenue dips (e.g., Sandesh: ₹292Cr vs ₹320Cr; DB Corp similar trends).
  • Cost Inflation: Newsprint/distribution costs rising; fixed costs compressing margins.
  • Competition: National players encroaching on regional markets; free digital alternatives eroding paid subscriptions.
Tailwinds (Positive Factors)
  • Regional Resilience: Strong loyalty in Tier-II/III markets (e.g., Gujarat for Sandesh/Dainik Bhaskar); hyperlocal content premium for advertisers.
  • Diversification Success: Hybrid models working—digital/OOH growing (OOH +10%, digital ads ₹700B); ancillary revenues (events, e-papers) offsetting print weakness.
  • Credibility Edge: High trust in print amid misinformation; premium demographics valued for ads.
  • Financial Stability: Debt-free (no borrowings), strong cash flows/investments (e.g., Sandesh EBITDA 32%, PAT margins 23-26%); high promoter holdings ensure stability.
Growth Prospects
  • Modest Sector Growth: Print CAGR 3-4% to ₹288B by 2026; overall M&E to ₹3T by 2027 (digital leading at 11% CAGR).
  • Digital Pivot: Subscriptions rising (video: 111M users); AI/personalization for ads/content; FAST/CTV expansion.
  • Regional/OOH Expansion: Tier-II/III demand; DOOH to 17% of OOH by 2027 (CAGR 24%).
  • Hybrid Monetization: Bundling print/digital/events; SME self-serve platforms; ₹1T digital ad potential by 2026.
  • Company-Specific: Sandesh/Jagran/DB Corp focusing on vernacular/hyperlocal; potential M&A for scale.
Key Risks
  • Competition/Disruption: Digital/national media eroding share; failure to adapt risks obsolescence.
  • Economic Sensitivity: Local downturns hit ads/subscriptions; inflation in inputs.
  • Regulatory/Legal: Defamation suits (e.g., Sandesh: ₹15Cr claim pending); policy changes (content/ad rules).
  • Tech/Operational: Delayed digital shift; cyber/investment valuation volatility (e.g., FY25 profit drop from unrealized gains).
  • Reputation: Misinformation backlash; maintaining independence amid pressures.

Overall Outlook: Print media resilient but transitioning to hybrid/digital models. Regional players like those analyzed show stability (debt-free, dividends), but growth hinges on digital acceleration amid modest 3-4% CAGR. High promoter control (e.g., 75-99% voting) ensures strategic continuity, but sector risks from digital shift persist.

Analysis of Indian Print Media (Using Jagran Prakashan Limited Q2FY26 Results as Proxy)

Jagran Prakashan Limited (JPL), a leading player in Indian print media with flagship Dainik Jagran, provides insights into the sector’s dynamics. Standalone print business shows resilience, but consolidated results highlight diversification challenges amid digital/radio headwinds. Below is a structured summary:

Tailwinds (Positive Drivers)

  • Robust Print Ad Growth: Standalone ad revenues +12.9% YoY (Q2) and +12.3% (H1) to Rs 528.8 cr, driven by Dainik Jagran (revenue +12.3% to Rs 299 cr, 23% margins). Hindi print remains strong in Tier 2/3 markets.
  • Core Print Stability: Dainik Jagran and other publications maintain healthy margins (~23% and 6-7%); circulation steady despite digital shift.
  • Diversification Gains: Other operating revenues +15% (events/outdoor), contributing to overall revenue growth (standalone +10.1% Q2).
  • One-off Boosts: High other income (e.g., Rs 31.8 cr Keyman policy maturity) lifted H1 PAT +45.7% standalone.
  • Brand Strength: IRS2019 confirms Dainik Jagran as India’s most-read newspaper; awards (e.g., E4M Maverick) enhance credibility.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Circulation Pressure: Flat/declining (Q2 standalone -1.8%, consolidated -2%); reflects subscriber shift to digital/free content.
  • Subsidiary Drag: Radio (Music Broadcast) revenue -31% YoY to Rs 37.8 cr, operating loss -14.5% margins (vs. +5.5% prior); digital (print) losses widened to -13.4% margins.
  • Slower Consolidated Growth: +4.7% Q2 revenues, flat operating profits; radio/digital eroded print gains.
  • Sector-Wide Ad Softness: Consolidated ads +4.7% (muted vs. standalone); Q1 radio/digital weakness persisted into Q2.
  • Cost Pressures: Expenses up in radio (e.g., depreciation/interest); other publications volatile (Q1 loss).

Growth Prospects

  • Print Core Expansion: Leverage Dainik Jagran/Naidunia dominance in Hindi heartland; potential ad recovery in festive/wedding seasons.
  • Digital Upside: Rs 21.7 cr revenue (flat but scalable); 65 Mn UVs (top-15 news category), with Hindi (41 Mn) and education (20 Mn) niches. Monetization via ads/programmatic could accelerate.
  • Non-Print Diversification: Outdoor/events +16% revenue; Jagran Engage pan-India footprint. Radio recovery possible via digital streaming (radiocity.in).
  • H1 Momentum: Standalone PAT +45.7%, EPS +45.5%; strong credit ratings (CRISIL AA+/Stable) support capex/debt for growth.
  • Sector Tailwinds: Rising rural ad spends, regional language demand; JPL’s 8 publications across 13 states position it well.

Key Risks

  • Digital Disruption: Accelerating shift eroding circulation/ads; digital losses (-Rs 2.9 cr OP Q2) signal monetization delays.
  • Radio Volatility: Revenue cliff (-31% YoY), losses mounting (-Rs 6.9 cr PAT Q2); FM license renewals/regulatory changes a threat.
  • Ad Cyclicality: Dependence on ads (63-70% revenues); economic slowdown/inflation could hit discretionary spends.
  • Subsidiary Dependence: Music Broadcast/Midday drag consolidated PAT (49% YoY growth but flat OP); integration risks.
  • Macro/External: Input cost inflation (newsprint), competition from digital natives (e.g., Google News), policy shifts (e.g., CSR mandates, data privacy).

Overall Summary: Indian print media shows resilience in core Hindi print (10%+ growth), buoyed by ad strength and brand moats, but faces secular headwinds from digital/radio (consolidated ~4% growth). Prospects hinge on digital monetization and diversification; risks center on subsidiary losses and ad volatility. JPL’s standalone strength suggests sector stability, with FY26 upside if radio stabilizes. Investors should monitor Q3 ads and digital margins.
