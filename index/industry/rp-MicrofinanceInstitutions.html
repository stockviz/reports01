Summary Analysis of Indian Microfinance Institutions (MFIs)

Based on Q2/H1 FY26 disclosures from CreditAccess Grameen, Muthoot Microfin, Spandana Sphoorty Financial (SSFL), Fusion Finance, and Satin Creditcare.

Headwinds (Challenges)

  • Portfolio Stress & Delinquencies: Industry-wide over-leveraging led to elevated GNPA (e.g., SSFL: 5.62% GNPA, 1.17% NNPA; slippages Rs396cr in Q2). X-bucket collections improved but dipped slightly in Oct due to festivals, rains/floods.
  • Slow Disbursement Recovery: Q1 FY26 disbursements muted (SSFL: Rs280cr); Q2 pickup (Rs934cr) but Oct lower. High rejection rates (65-80%) due to MFIN guardrails (e.g., 1-2 lender limits, zero/30 DPD filters).
  • Financial Strain: Losses reported (SSFL: Rs249cr net loss, negative PPOP Rs40cr) from interest reversals, high provisions/write-offs (Rs86cr technical), and OPEX amid AUM contraction (SSFL: Rs4,088cr).
  • Operational Adjustments: Branch closures/mergers (SSFL: 101 branches), elevated credit costs (2.5-3% expected FY27).

Tailwinds (Positive Factors)

  • Improving Collections & Quality: X-bucket efficiency at 98.5-98.7% (Sep-Oct), up across states (e.g., Karnataka normalized; Bihar 98.8%). New FY26 book strong (0.1% delinquency, 99.9% collections).
  • Recovery Momentum: Initiatives like demand/legal notices (90k/3.3L issued), Lok Adalat (8.6k cases), digital collections yielding Rs7.7cr recoveries since Aug.
  • Liquidity & Capital Strength: High cash buffers (SSFL: 21% of assets, Rs1,270cr Oct-end), CRAR 47%, gearing 1.5x; debt raised Rs598cr. Rights issue closed successfully.
  • Productivity Gains: Per LO disbursements up via tech (upfront bureau checks), weekly branches; NIM/yield improved (8.4%/19.6%).
  • Active Investor Engagement: Multiple group meetings (e.g., CreditAccess Dec 3, Muthoot Dec 1, Satin Dec 4) signal confidence; no UPSI shared.

Growth Prospects

  • Disbursement Ramp-Up: SSFL targets Rs500cr+ monthly (Q3), Rs700cr+ run-rate (Q4); FY26 new book at 37% of BS by Oct-end. Rs6,000cr opportunity from 10L existing customers (76% with ≤2 lenders).
  • AUM Trajectory: SSFL expects sequential AUM growth from Q3 (flattish FY26 YoY); FY27 ~20% growth. New customer enrollment rising (22% of Q2 loans).
  • Diversification: SSFL’s Criss Financial (Rs671cr AUM) shifting to secured Micro LAP (39% now, target 50-55%); industry macros (good monsoon) support rural demand.
  • Sector Rebuild: Guardrails reducing leverage; potential CGFMU adoption for stable costs; yield expansion as new book grows.

Key Risks

  • External Shocks: Floods (AP, Bihar, Odisha, WB), festivals/elections (Bihar), fewer working days impacting collections/disbursals.
  • High Rejections & Acquisition: Stringent filters hinder new customers; over-reliance on existing base (43% unserved eligible).
  • Credit Costs & Write-Offs: Forward flows to 90+ at ~Rs150cr/Q (down from Rs360cr); multi-lender book (17-23%) prone to delinquencies.
  • State/Geography Variance: Laggards like AP (floods); unsecured segments stressed (e.g., SSFL Criss unsecured).
  • Regulatory/Execution: MFIN guardrail persistence; covenant breaches possible if recovery slows; MD/CEO vacancy at SSFL (announcement in 30 days).
  • Macro Sensitivity: Rural over-indebtedness relapse if macros weaken.

Overall Outlook: Sector in “rebuild” phase post-stress; tailwinds from collections/quality gaining traction outweigh headwinds. FY26 stabilization, FY27 growth resumption likely, but execution on disbursals/recoveries critical. Strong liquidity buffers mitigate near-term risks.
