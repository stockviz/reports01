Indian Computers - Software & Consulting Sector Analysis (Based on Q2/H1 FY26 Earnings Transcripts)

The sector exhibits resilient but cautious performance amid macroeconomic headwinds (US tariffs, H1B visa changes, elections) and AI-driven disruption. Revenue growth moderated (flat to mid-single digits Q-o-Q for most), with TCV/deal wins strong but conversion lagged due to decision delays and client rationalization. AI optimism dominates, with firms reporting 10-28% AI-influenced bookings/pipeline. Margins improved (mid-teens EBITDA) via utilization/offshoring/AI productivity. Guidance: 3-5% FY26 growth (industry avg.), selective optimism for H2 recovery.

Headwinds

  • Macro/Policy Uncertainty: US tariffs, protectionism, H1B visa hikes (minimal direct impact <3-5% headcount exposure; indirect via client caution). Furloughs loom in Q3 (BFSI/TMT heavy).
  • Client Spending Caution: Optimization, vendor consolidation, lengthening cycles (12-18 months). Ramp-downs in large TMT/BFSI clients (e.g., 50% BFSI drop in some).
  • Vertical Weakness: TMT/Hi-Tech secular slowdown (CapEx shift to GPUs/AI infra, layoffs); Manufacturing/Retail/Auto muted (policy/tariff noise).
  • Seasonality/Execution: Q3 weak (furloughs); TCV-to-revenue lag (1-2Q); flat YoY TCV/book-to-bill ~1x.

Tailwinds

  • Margin Resilience: Mid-teens EBITDA (up 20-160bps Q-o-Q) via 84-87% utilization, offshore mix (53-57%), AI productivity (20-50% gains in SDLC/ops), cost controls. Wage hikes absorbed.
  • AI Momentum: 10-28% AI bookings/pipeline; platforms (NeoIP, ZenseAI, AI Force) driving wins. Productivity offsets headcount flatness.
  • Deal Momentum: Strong TCV ($158-528M/Q; H1 >FY25 full-year for some); large deals (6-10/H1) in BFSI/HLS.
  • Cash/Financial Health: High OCF (74-133% EBITDA conversion), dividends (quarterly policy emerging), DSO 55-89 days.

Growth Prospects

  • AI/Modernization: Core driver (28-69% pipeline AI-led); agentic AI, legacy mod, cloud/data factories. FY26 guidance: 3-5% (HCLTech/Aurionpro); 20-30% aspirational (smaller firms).
  • Verticals/Geos: BFSI/HLS (13-32% YoY, 33% rev mix); TMT recovery via mid-tech. NA (70% rev), Europe/Australia expansion.
  • Order Book/Pipeline: 1.02-1.28 book-to-bill; record pipelines (97-139% YoY); H2 > H1 expected (ramp-ups).
  • Structural: Offshore-led (GDCaaS), subscription/SaaS shift, M&A (capability buys $10-15M rev).

Key Risks

  • Client Concentration: Ramp-downs in top 1-2 clients (TMT/BFSI); 53-58% top-10 rev.
  • Vertical/Geopolitical: TMT prolonged weakness; US policy (tariffs/H1B delay deals).
  • Execution: TCV lag (1-2Q ramp); Q3 furloughs; AI cannibalization (10-50% productivity hit).
  • Competition: Vendor consolidation favors scale; pricing pressure in non-AI.
  • Macro: Discretionary deferral; talent localization costs.

Overall Outlook: Sector pivots to AI-led efficiency/modernization for 5-10% FY26 growth (above historical 4-6%). Smaller/mid-caps (20-30% aspirational) agile via AI/offshore; large-caps stable. Risks skewed to US/TMT, balanced by AI tailwinds. Monitor H2 TCV conversion/Q3 furloughs.
